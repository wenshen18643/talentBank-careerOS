# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two roles share one platform, but the prototype builds the **candidate** spine first:

- **Candidates / professionals** — people thinking about their career as a long arc, not a single application. They want to log work as it happens and understand the realistic range of paths open to them. Context: reflective, often mid-career, allergic to hype.
- **Recruiters / employers** (later phase) — want to find the right person before they're publicly looking, with a reason for the match, not spam. Context: scanning a curated pipeline, deciding who to reach.

The job to be done: keep an honest, continuous record of what you actually did, and be connected to the right opportunity at the right moment — for a reason you can read.

## Product Purpose

Career OS is a career operating system built on two bets that traditional job boards get wrong:

1. **Continuous logging over retroactive CVs.** The "Living Ledger" is a running record of projects, decisions, and leadership moments. An AI "Improve & Expand" engine (Kimi, with a deterministic offline fallback) turns rough logs into CV-ready bullets without inventing a single metric.
2. **Forward-looking trajectory matching over backward keyword matching.** Candidates stay invisible until they genuinely match an employer's criteria and consent to surface. Matches carry a human-readable reason, never a black-box score.

Success looks like: a candidate who has logged real work, refined it into a truthful CV, and trusts that the system reads where they're going — not just where they've been.

## Brand Personality

Calm and considered. Quiet confidence, editorial restraint, generous whitespace. The voice is plain and human — it explains its reasoning and names its uncertainty rather than performing precision. Three words: **honest, continuous, considered.** It should feel like a long-term companion for your career, not a transactional recruiting tool.

## Anti-references

- **Legacy job boards** (Indeed / Monster / LinkedIn density) — blue corporate chrome, banner ads, spammy outreach.
- **Generic SaaS template** — cream background, gradient hero, identical feature-card grid, tiny uppercase eyebrows above every section.
- **Black-box AI scoring** — big confidence numbers, opaque match percentages, dashboards of meaningless metrics.
- **Cold ATS / HR tooling** (Workday / Greenhouse) — enterprise grayness, form-heavy, soulless.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the reasoning, not a score.** Every recommendation or match states why and where the uncertainty sits.
2. **Never fabricate.** The AI restructures and sharpens what the user actually said; it never invents a metric or achievement.
3. **Hold the long view.** Read any single step in the context of a multi-decade arc, not in isolation.
4. **Consent is the gate.** A candidate is never dumped into a pipeline or contacted without an explicit yes.
5. **Friction-free in, deliberate out.** Logging is instant and rough; refinement and outreach are explicit, visible steps.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA. Body text meets ≥4.5:1 contrast; large text ≥3:1. Full keyboard navigation, visible focus rings, semantic landmarks. Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` alternative. Color is never the sole carrier of meaning (status is labelled in text as well as tinted).
